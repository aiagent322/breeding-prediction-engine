Performance Summary

Lone Woolf 'Ace' -- 2020 Red Roan Stallion. AQHA/APHA (solid Paint). By Metallic Rebel (Metallic Cat x Sweet Abra/Abrakadabracre -- LTE $438,315, NCHA Open Horse of the Year 2017, NCHA Hall of Fame, NCHA World Champion Stallion 13 championships, PE $16.6M+, NRCHA Million Dollar Sire -- full profile bvss004) out of A Little Reylena (Dual Rey x Katie Little Lena/Smart Little Lena -- LTE $126,058; EquiStat Elite $1 Million Dam; QData and NCHA Top 15 All-Time Leading Dam per Valley Equine 2026; dam of Dual Reyish $452,687 [2017 NCHA Open Futurity Champion, PE $2.5M+, NCHA Hall of Fame -- by Hottish, bvss005] AND Lone Woolf [2023 NCHA Open Futurity Champion] -- two separate NCHA Open Futurity Champions from the same dam in a six-year span, one of the most extraordinary produce records in cutting horse history; A Little Reylena's confirmed show record includes 2008 NCHA Summer Spectacular 5/6YO 50K NP Champion, 2008 Utah Futurity 5/6YO NP Co-Champion, 2009 NCHA Super Stakes 5/6YO NP Reserve Champion, 2009 The Mane Event 5/6YO NP Reserve Champion). Bred and owned by Billy Wolf, Whitesboro TX (Wolf Livestock). Shown by Wesley Galyean, EquiStat Elite $6.8M+ Rider, Claremore OK -- Galyean persuaded Wolf not to sell Lone Woolf as a yearling at the Western Bloodstock sale after seeing him work in a round pen: 'He was so fast so light footed, just insane talent and I immediately went and told Billy I said, Listen, this is the type of horse that you are going to turn around in a year or two and try to buy back for a couple of million dollars.' Wolf did not sell, and Galyean's assessment proved correct. NCHA Hall of Fame. Stud fee $5,000. Eligible: Valley Equine Super Stallion Incentive, Breeders Invitational, NRCHA Cow Horse Incentive, NCHA Super Stakes, PCCHA, Chrome Cash. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX. Semen availability: GREY designation on Valley Equine Breeding Calendar. LTE $668,310 (Valley Equine 2026 -- most current confirmed). Highest earning offspring of Metallic Rebel and highest earning offspring of A Little Reylena -- both confirmed Valley Equine 2026 lineup. 2023 NCHA Open Futurity Champion: scored 228.5 as the last horse to enter the pen at the Will Rogers Coliseum in Fort Worth TX on December 9, 2023, besting over 500 horses entered -- the largest NCHA Futurity ever hosted. Won $318,649.29 plus incentives including Chrome Cash ($6,534). This gave Wesley Galyean his second NCHA Open Futurity Championship -- his first was in 2004 aboard Spots Hot as the youngest rider ever to win the title. NCHA: 'God is Great,' Galyean proclaimed repeatedly when crowned champion. NCHA Reserve Horse of the Year -- confirmed Valley Equine 2026. NCHA Hall of Fame Inductee -- confirmed Valley Equine 2026. Placed 2nd in the 2024 NCHA Super Stallion Incentive at the NCHA Futurity (paid $6,896) -- confirmed Valley Equine Summer Spectacular article. As a 4-year-old in 2024 QHN reported: 'He is already grasped more champion and reserve champion titles and is now on his way to the NCHA Super Stakes Semi Finals.' Valley Equine 2026: 'Lone Woolf earned his place among the most accomplished horses of his generation through sustained success at the highest level of competition.' No offspring data yet -- first foals not yet of showing age.
