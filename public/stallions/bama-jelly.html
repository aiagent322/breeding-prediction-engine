Performance Summary

Bama Jelly — 2015 Bay. By Bamacat (High Brow Cat x MH San Tules Dually/San Tule Freckles — LTE $352,857, NCHA World Champion Stallion, 7-Panel Negative, PE $2.5M, standing at 6666 Ranch — "consistently sires performers that excel in cutting and roping events" and "has proven to pass that cow sense and ability to his foals") out of Wood She Be Neat (Nitas Wood x unknown). Bamacat carries High Brow Cat on his top side and Freckles Playboy, Dual Pep, and Peppy San Badger on the dam side through MH San Tules Dually (LTE $331,846 — NCHA Silver Award, Millionheir Non-Pro Classic Champion, dam of 18 money-earners totaling $906,716). This gives Bamacat's offspring a combination of High Brow Cat speed and cow with Dual Pep athletic grit. Bama Fury, a full sibling to Bamacat out of a related dam, won the Royal Crown Roping Heading Futurity confirming the roping cross potential in this sire line. Nitas Wood (Wood She Be Neat's sire): 1995 Sorrel, Zack T Wood x Nitas Quixote (Doc Quixote) — EquiStat Elite $6 Million Sire described as the "ultimate outcross." His offspring include Woody Be Lucky ($705K NCHA HoF), Junie Wood ($541K NCHA HoY — dam of 2021 NCHA Futurity Champion Janie Wood by Stevie Rey Von, creating a powerful nick), Woodys Baby ($388K, NCHA Non-Pro World Champion), Woodys Bad Boy ($369K NCHA Non-Pro Futurity Champion), and Woody Be Tuff ($351K, PE $15.3M+). Wood She Be Neat herself earned $27,600 and was shown in Amateur and Non-Pro by Madalyn Colgrove. Her dam produce now exceeds $838,000 with two standouts: Bama Jelly ($441K) and Crimsonnette ($111K, NCHA Limited Open Futurity Champion, Augusta Non-Pro Classic winner). LTE $441,762. NCHA Hall of Fame (inducted 2023). 2021 NCHA World Champion Stallion. Scored 230 with Austin Shepard (NCHA Hall of Fame earner $8.37M) to win the Southern Futurity 5/6 Open Classic. Full show record: Augusta Open Futurity Champion (4YO), Arbuckle Mountain Open Futurity Champion (4YO), Southern Open Classic Champion (5/6YO, 228 score), BI Open Classic Challenge Champion (2021), 2021 and 2022 NCHA Open Finals Reserve Champion, 2021 NCHA Eastern Nationals Open Reserve Champion, Split 4th NCHA Open Super Stakes, 2018 Futurity finalist, 2019 Super Stakes Open finalist, 2021-22 NCHA Open Top 15. Described: "very smart about a cow, with amazing talent, athleticism, and genetics." First crop 4-year-olds 2025: RT Playin My Jam placed 2nd at the 2026 NCHA Super Stakes 4-Year-Old Open (221 score). Stud fee $3,500. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX.
