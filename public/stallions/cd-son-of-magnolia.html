Performance Summary

CD Son Of Magnolia — 2003 Sorrel, 14.2H, 1050 lbs. By CD Olena (LTE $170,706 — 1994 NCHA Open Futurity Champion shown by Todd Schlup; 1995 NCHA Horse of the Year; QData and NCHA #5 All-Time Leading Sire with 535 money-earners and PE $14.1M; he was described by trainers as a bright-lights horse who elevated his performance when championship money was on the line — stepping up in go-rounds and finals that mattered most) out of Eye Of The Magnolia (by Smell The Magnolias). CD Olena traces directly to two of the industry's most foundational stallions. His sire Doc O'Lena (Doc Bar x Poco Lena/Poco Bueno — AQHA and NCHA Hall of Fame, 1970 NCHA Futurity Champion under Shorty Freeman) sired Smart Little Lena — the first NCHA Triple Crown winner, #2 All-Time Leading NCHA Sire with $39M+ in offspring, who was syndicated for $2.1 million in 1978, a record at that time for the cutting horse industry. Doc O'Lena's own trainer Shorty Freeman described him as "a thinking horse with a natural connection with the cow." CD Olena's dam is CD Chica San Badger by Peppy San Badger (LTE $279,341 — King Ranch bred, first NCHA World Champion to sire another World Champion, PE $28M+; his get include Little Pepto Gal $526K NCHA HoY, Copaspepto $480K NCHA HoY, Peptoboonsmal $180K NCHA Futurity Champion). This gives CD Olena direct access to both the Doc Bar (via Doc O'Lena) and King Ranch Peppy San Badger (via CD Chica San Badger) lines — two of the pillars of foundation cutting genetics. CD Olena daughters confirmed elite producers: they produced High Brow CD (2007 NCHA Futurity Champion under Beau Galyean, 2008 NCHA Horse of the Year, PE $14.1M+) confirming the line's generational elite production continues. LTE $69,963. Owned by Beggs Cattle Company. The value of CD Son Of Magnolia is concentrated on the top side of his pedigree. The Doc O'Lena tail-male line through CD Olena is rare in the modern cutting horse gene pool — most contemporary horses trace Doc O'Lena through daughters rather than sons, making this stallion a genuine outcross for the dominant Metallic Cat, High Brow Cat, and Dual Rey bloodlines that saturate today's cutting industry. Breeders seeking authentic Doc Bar and Doc O'Lena tail-male genetics — while crossing on modern dominant dam-side bloodlines — find this stallion an unusual and biologically legitimate option. The dam line through Smell The Magnolias is not a documented elite performance sire, placing this breeding value firmly in the top half of the pedigree. Peppy San Badger on the second cross adds additional foundation King Ranch genetics that many modern breeders actively seek to reintroduce. Standing at 6666 Ranch, Guthrie TX.
