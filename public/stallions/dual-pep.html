Performance Summary

Dual Pep -- 1985 Chestnut Stallion, deceased 2018. ICSI only. 15H. 5-panel Negative (HYPP N/N, PSSM N/N, HERDA N/N, GBED N/N, MH N/N). Bred by Nic-A-Lode Farms, Boise ID. By Peppy San Badger (Mr San Peppy x Leo San/Peppy Belle -- legendary all-time leading cutting horse sire; sire of Dual Pep, Smart Little Lena, Colonel Freckles, and dozens of all-time leading earners and sires) out of Miss Dual Doc (Doc's Remedy x -- Doc's Remedy is a Doc Bar son; dam's cutting credentials established in NCHA). Owned by Hill Ranch. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX. Stud fee $2,500 ICSI only confirmed Valley Equine 2026 lineup and StallionCompare. 77% foundation bloodlines. Sire of Dual Rey (bvss001 in this database -- LTE $105,038, PE $57,893,606, QData and NCHA #3 All-Time Leading Cutting Sire, deceased 2018) making Dual Pep the only All-Time Top 5 Leading Sire with a son also on the All-Time Top 5 list confirmed NCHA obituary September 2018. LTE $313,192 confirmed Valley Equine 2026 (NCHA portion $302,053.60 confirmed NCHA obituary and StallionCompare). Confirmed show record: 1998 NCHA World Champion Cutting confirmed StallionCompare; 1989 Memphis Futurity Open Reserve Champion confirmed NCHA obituary ('Dual Pep claimed the open reserve championship of the 1989 Memphis Futurity'); NCHA Open Classic Champion confirmed StallionCompare. Shown by Pat Earnheart in open, Bobby Pidgeon in Non-Pro. By end of show career at age 6, NCHA earnings stood at $302,054 confirmed NCHA obituary. NCHA obit: 'Year after year, for more than two decades, Dual Pep offspring dominated the cutting charts, as do the offspring of his sons and daughters, today.' On September 23, 2018, days before his death, Dual Pep's daughter Tears From Heaven won the 2018 Brash Bash -- making Dual Pep the oldest living sire ever to have a major cutting futurity champion at time of death. PE $26,186,416 confirmed Valley Equine 2026. QData and NCHA #7 All-Time Leading Cutting Sire confirmed Valley Equine 2026. QData #6 All-Time Leading Cutting Maternal Grandsire -- daughters produced more than $44 Million in earnings and over 10,000 AQHA points confirmed Valley Equine 2026. Top offspring: Dual Rey Me LTE $812,942 -- 2-time NCHA Open World Champion (2006 and 2007) and 3-time NCHA Open Finals Champion (2006, 2007, 2009) confirmed Valley Equine 2026 and NCHA obituary; Dual Rey LTE $105,038 PE $57,893,606 QData #3 All-Time Leading Cutting Sire (bvss001 -- full profile); three NCHA Non-Pro Horses of the Year confirmed Valley Equine 2026. Incentives: Breeders Invitational and NCHA Super Stakes. Valley Equine Super Stallion Incentive not listed for Dual Pep foals in available sources.
