Full Profile

Cool N Hot "Trigger" — 2014 Chestnut, 14.1H, 1022 lbs. By Hottish (Spots Hot x Stylish Play Lena/Docs Stylish Oak — LTE $301,843, 2012 NCHA Open Derby Champion, 2012 NCHA Breeders Invitational Derby Open Champion, 2012 NCHA Summer Spectacular Derby Open Champion, 2012 NCHA Reserve Horse of the Year, 2025 QData #4 and NCHA #3 Leading Sire, NCHA Top 10 All-Time Leading Cutting Sire, PE $24.3M+; top offspring: Cool N Hot $435K NCHA HoY, Dual Reyish $452K NCHA Futurity Champion, Phil N Dangerous NCHA Open Futurity Champion, Kittennish NCHA Million Dollar Mare, Hott Rod NRCHA World's Greatest Horseman Champion) out of Donas Cool Cat (High Brow Cat x Sweet Peppy Again/Peppy San Badger — LTE $318,908, two-time NCHA Non-Pro World Finals Show Champion, QData and NCHA Top 15 All-Time Leading Dam, dam of 2012 NCHA Non-Pro Futurity Champion Donas Suen Boon $314,269 and Cool N Hot $435K). LTE $435,069. PE $526,820.

NCHA Hall of Fame. 2018 NCHA Open Horse of the Year. Owned by Kobie and Paula Wood of Stephenville, Texas — bred, raised, and shown by Kobie Wood (lifetime earnings $5.7M+, multiple NCHA World Championships) with Paula Wood riding in Non-Pro (EquiStat Elite $2 Million Rider). The only horse to make both the Open and Non-Pro finals at the 2018 NCHA Super Stakes.

Stud fee $3,000. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX. Confirmed show record: 2017 NCHA Futurity 3YO Non-Pro 5th; 2017 Bonanza Derby Open Champion, Non-Pro Champion; 2018 NCHA Super Stakes 4YO Open Derby Champion (scored 227 — Kobie Wood called it "the best run I've ever had in this pen," winning by a 4.5-point margin); 2018 NCHA Super Stakes 4YO Non-Pro Finalist (tied 12th, 212 score); 2018 Bonanza Derby Open Co-Champion; 2018 NCHA Open Horse of the Year; 2019 NCHA Summer Spectacular Open Classic Finalist; 2020 NCHA Summer Spectacular Open Classic Senior Champion; 2020 NCHA Cattlemens Open Classic Finalist; 2020 The Ike Open Classic Finalist; 2020 Abilene Spectacular Open Classic Finalist; 2020 Brazos Bash Open Classic Finalist. Multiple time Open and Non-Pro weekend champion throughout career.

Hottish's sire Spots Hot (LTE $529,435 — 2004 NCHA Open Futurity Champion, 2004 NCHA Horse of the Year, NCHA Hall of Fame, PE $2M+) passes the foundational Freckles Playboy/Doc O'Lena genetics from the Stylish Play Lena female family — the same dam line that produced Smooth Talkin Style (2015 NCHA HoY, $305K), Pepto Boom ($316K), Copperish ($281K), and Areybe ($160K). Donas Cool Cat's dam Sweet Peppy Again (Peppy San Badger) is the source of a collateral linebreeding pattern to Sugar Bars and Leo through multiple full and three-quarter brothers in the pedigree — a pattern documented in QHN's "In the Blood" profile. Cool N Hot's pedigree thus carries Peppy San Badger through both the Stylish Play Lena line (via Freckles Playboy) and the Donas Cool Cat line (via Sweet Peppy Again) creating a concentration of Peppy San Badger and Freckles Playboy genetics within a High Brow Cat framework. PE $526,820 with offspring earning in both cutting and reined cow horse across multiple divisions.

Valley Equine 2026: "proving to be the kind of sire breeders can build around — offspring showing up as earners in both cutting and cow horse in multiple divisions, fitting a variety of programs." NRCHA Cow Horse Incentive and Gold Buckle Futurities enrolled.
