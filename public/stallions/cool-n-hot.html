Full Profile

Cool N Hot 'Trigger' -- 2014 Chestnut Stallion, 14.1H, 1022 lbs. By Hottish (Spots Hot x Stylish Play Lena/Docs Stylish Oak -- PE $23.8M+, 2025 QData #3 Leading Sire, 2024 NCHA #4 Leading Sire, NRCHA Million Dollar Sire, deceased 2020 ICSI only -- full profile bvss005) out of Donas Cool Cat (High Brow Cat x Sweet Peppy Again/Peppy San Badger -- LTE $318,908; 2007 NCHA World Finals Non-Pro 3rd place; NCHA Youth World Championship with Lane Wood; confirmed QData and NCHA Top 15 All-Time Leading Dam of cutting money-earners per Valley Equine 2026; dam produce nearly $900,000 including Cool N Hot and Donas Suen Boon -- 2013 NCHA Futurity Non-Pro Champion, 2014 NCHA Super Stakes Classic Non-Pro Champion, and 2x AQHA World Champion in cutting with Kobie). Sweet Peppy Again (Donas Cool Cat's dam, Peppy San Badger x Sweet Sugar Again/Sugar Again): LTE $13,602, dam produce $442,179 including Scat Daddy $62,295 full brother to Donas Cool Cat. QHN pedigree analysis confirms linebreeding pattern in this pedigree: 'three-quarter brothers' pattern through Sugar Bars and Leo cross with Doc O'Lena blood, the same collateral linebreeding found in elite cutting producers.

Bred and owned by Kobie Wood (NCHA Elite $5 Million Rider, trainer/rider) and Paula Wood (registered owner/breeder, NCHA Elite $2 Million Rider, non-pro competitor). NCHA Hall of Fame inductee. Stud fee $3,000. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX.

LTE $435,069. 2018 NCHA Open Horse of the Year. NCHA Hall of Fame. Confirmed show record: 2018 NCHA Super Stakes Open Champion (scored 227 with Kobie Wood, $69,076 -- QHN: 'they set the arena abuzz when they marked a 227 for the title'); 2018 NCHA Open Horse of the Year; 2018 NCHA Super Stakes Non-Pro finals with Paula Wood (tied 12th, $13,337 earned); 2018 Brazos Bash Open Derby Co-Champion.

The Super Stakes Open Championship win was historic within the Hottish sire record -- it made Hottish the sire of the winners of two consecutive NCHA Triple Crown events: Dual Reyish won the 2017 NCHA Futurity and Cool N Hot won the 2018 Super Stakes. Kobie Wood had been in the winner's circle many times as an Elite $5 Million Rider, but this was his first NCHA Super Stakes Open Championship. PE $526,820 (Valley Equine 2026 -- early stage sire with first foals just now showing). Valley Equine 2026: 'Cool N Hot is proving to be the kind of sire breeders can build around.

His offspring are showing up as earners in both cutting and cow horse in multiple divisions, fitting a variety of programs. That comes as no surprise given his foundation. He is out of Donas Cool Cat, a QData and NCHA Top 15 All-Time Leading Dam of cutting money-earners, and that production strength is carrying forward.' Gold Buckle Futurities: 'Cool N Hot, a NCHA Horse of the Year and Hall of Fame inductee, earned over $435,000 in the show pen. Now standing at Valley Equine Reproduction, his offspring are proving just as competitive in the show pen.' The convergence of Hottish (elite athleticism and trainability sire) over a High Brow Cat daughter from one of the industry's top producing families creates a cross with generational production depth on both sides -- Hottish's dam Stylish Play Lena is QData #4 All-Time Leading Cutting Dam, and Donas Cool Cat is QData/NCHA Top 15 All-Time Leading Dam -- giving Cool N Hot perhaps the strongest dam-line pedigree of any active Valley Equine stallion.
