Full Profile

Smart Chic Olena 'Chic' -- 1985 Sorrel Stallion. By Smart Little Lena (Doc O'Lena x Smart Peppy/Peppy San -- NCHA Triple Crown winner; #2 All-Time Leading Cutting Sire PE $42M+; Doc O'Lena 1970 NCHA Futurity Champion; Peppy San 1967 NCHA World Champion Cutting Horse, AQHA Hall of Fame; Smart Little Lena himself was the NCHA Triple Crown winner earning $743,275 -- first horse ever to achieve it) out of Gay Sugar Chic (Gay Bar King x Gay Widow -- Gay Bar King by Three Bars TB, AQHA Hall of Fame sire of 5 AQHA Champions, offspring NCHA earnings $732K+; Gay Widow AQHA Champion, superior in halter; Gay Sugar Chic produce earnings $401,895, full sister to winner of 1981 NCHA Non-Pro Derby; TRJ confirms Three Bars and Chicaro TB ancestors on bottom side bring speed genetics). Bred by Emily Woodall, New Caney TX. Purchased for $25,000 by Bill Glass during the recession.

Then purchased by Jim Babcock of Texas. Trainers in cutting: Bill Glass, Bill Freeman, Dell Bell, Randy Butler. Reining trainer: Craig Johnson. Jim Babcock: 'I paid $25,000 for him during the recession -- so I gambled on Chic, but he never made me sorry for it.' Died June 24, 2012, at age 27, owned by Bill Richardson, Whitesboro TX.

Semen and breeding rights acquired October 2015 by Dr. Trevor and Tiffiny Bond, Smokin Gun Performance Horses, Mound Valley KS. 5-Panel Negative. Extremely limited -- ICSI only, with rare frozen semen available; all breeding managed through Valley Equine.

AQHA Hall of Fame. NRHA Hall of Fame (2004). NRCHA Hall of Fame. Valley Equine, Stephenville TX.

LTE $167,472. The ONLY horse in history to win AQHA World Championships in both cutting and reining. Full confirmed show record: 1988 NCHA Futurity Open -- advanced to semifinals (lost a cow in semis, shown by Bill Glass); 1989 NCHA Super Stakes Open 3rd place (scored 220, earned $32,000+, shown by both Bill Glass and Bill Freeman); 1990 AQHA World Champion Senior Cutting Horse; 1990 The Non-Pro Open Cutting Co-Champion; 1990 Bonanza Open Classic 5th; 1990 NCHA Open Finals top 10; 1991 Congress Open Cutting Champion; 1991 AQHA World Champion Senior Reining (after retiring from cutting); 1993 AQHA World Champion Senior Reining; 1993 NRHA Open Reserve World Champion; 1993 Congress NRHA Open Champion; 1993 NRHA Derby Open Champion. Breeding fee: started at $750 as an unknown quantity; rose to $25,000 by age 20 -- entirely on the merit of his offspring.

H&R: 'He's just an awfully smart, awfully good horse.' His foals were predominantly sorrels and chestnuts, many with blaze faces and characteristic roaning patterns -- Babcock: 'Chic proved a dominant sire from the start. His foals all looked alike.' PE $15,449,532 (Valley Equine 2026). First appeared on leading sire list in 1995 with just two foal crops showing. Became the #1 All-Time Leading Sire.

QData Top 5 All-Time Leading Reined Cow Horse Sire. Top 10 All-Time Leading Reining Sire. Top 10 All-Time Leading Ranch Riding Sire -- confirmed Valley Equine 2026. 26 confirmed earners of more than $100,000.

NRCHA Million Dollar Sire (2004). 2,077 registered AQHA foals, 1,037 money earners. Prepotency stretched nearly 3 decades from 1991 through 2019. Top confirmed offspring: Smart Spook ($405,650 LTE -- 2004 NRHA Futurity Open Champion with Shawn Flarida; EquiStat Elite $6 Million Sire himself); Olena Oak ($381,390 LTE, NRCHA portion $346,398 -- highest-earning NRCHA horse at time of documentation, also earning in cutting and reining); Chics Magic Potion ($246,705 NRCHA LTE); A Chic In Time ($300K+ earner); Smart Crackin Chic ($191,375 NRCHA); Travelin Jonez ($174,785 NRCHA).

QHN: 'There is no doubt Chic made a lasting impact on the Western performance industry when he showed that competing at a high level in multiple disciplines was possible.' TRJ: 'Some stallions win. A rare few transform the direction of an entire industry. Smart Chic Olena did both. He remains the only horse in history to win AQHA world championships in both cutting and reining.' His significance as a maternal grandsire is equally confirmed: his daughters have produced elite performers across cutting, reining, cow horse, barrel racing, and team roping.
